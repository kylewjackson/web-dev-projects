The Movie The Show Cast and Crew Ratings and Awards Impact

The Movie

  • 1992
  • Dir Fran Rubel Kuzui
  • Writer Joss Whedon
  • Starring Kristy Swanson, Donald Sutherland, and Luke Perry

Whedon attempted to subvert the "blonde cheerleader damel-in-distress" trope common in horror films, with the gimmick being she was what the monsters should ultimately fear.

The show follows the events of the movie, in that it presents Buffy's life following her family moving to Sunnydale, but the movie itself is not considered canon.

A comic book adapation of the movie called Buffy Origins was released in subsequent years, and more closely resmembles Whedon's original script. While not written by himself, Whedon has stated that, aside from a few details, the comic is mostly canon.

Whedon was ultimately disatisfied with alterations made to his script, which included changes to tone, dialogue, and plot. He became frustrated and walked off set, disowning the project entirely.

The Show

  • Created by Joss Whedon
  • 1997 - 2003 (144 episodes)
  • The WB/UPN

Follows Buffy after moving to Sunnydale, and her journey fighting vampires, demons, and the forces of darkness.

Gail Berman approached Whedon to produce a non-broadcast pilot to rectify the film's wrongs, and it was picked up by The WB

Descirbed as "My So-Called Life" meets "The X-Files" and utilized a "High School is Hell" metaphor.

Cast and Crew

  • Joss Whedon
  • Sarah Michelle Gellar
  • Marti Noxon

Whedon executive produced, along with Kuzui Enterprises, Sandollar TV, and Twentieth Century Fox.

Kuzui was credited with production on the show, but only held the film rights, and had no actual involvment in the direction of the show.

Helped launch the careers of many actors, writers, and directors. Drew Goddard is very succesful, with his movie recently releasing to critical acclaim.

Ratings and Awards

  • Golden Globes
  • Emmys
  • 7.9 mil

The show was critically acclaimed and nominated for many awards over its run, including Emmys and Golden Globes, winning in categories mostly pertaining to writing.

Buffy was the second show to win the TV Critics Association Heritage Award, after The Simpsons.

Buffy did moderately well in the ratings, especially for the fledgling WB network, but would never truly compete with bigger network shows. However, it ranked very highly on the WB itself, and later UPN.

Impact

  • Merchandise
  • Buffy Studies
  • Comics

Buffy has inspired tons of merchandise, been the subject of several podcasts, and is constantly being referenced as one of the most influential programs to have aired on television.

Buffy in the context of 2018 is sometimes problematic, in terms of racial diversity, bisexual erasure, and feminism, but as long as those issues are acknowledged, I think the show can still be enjoyed.

A reboot is planned, having been annouced in July 2018. Monica Owusu-Breen will be showrunning, with Joss Whedon producing. The showrunner has confirmed that the show will serve as a continuation, rather than a remake.
